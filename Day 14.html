From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In grammar, an article is a word that is used with a noun to identify the object or idea. Articles are a part of speech.

Articles combine with nouns to form noun phrases and show definiteness. In English, the is the definite article, while a and an are indefinite articles.

Types of Article [ edit ]

Definite Article [ edit ]

"Definite article" refers to a specific noun that is already known.

A definite article is used to refer to a particular person, place, thing, or idea. In English, the definite article is the.

For example, compare the following sentences:

  1. She found the key that she lost yesterday.
  2. She found a key on the road.

In sentence 1, the refers to a specific key already known. In sentence 2, a refers to any key.

The definite article can also represent an entire class or species:

The lion is known as the king of the jungle.

Here, the does not mean one lion but all lions as a group.
